The Wind Hunter

Chapter One

The Girl Who Chose the Wrong Dragon

The storm arrived before sunrise.

Black clouds rolled over the kingdom of Hiyanthiea, swallowing the stars one by one. Thunder shook the palace towers while rain lashed against stained-glass windows. Most of the kingdom still slept, hidden beneath warm blankets and peaceful dreams.

But Keila Ravaryn stood wide awake at the edge of the palace roof.

Wind tore through her dark braid as she stared toward the distant mountains known as the Teeth of Veyr. Somewhere beyond those jagged peaks, wild dragons roamed free—far from royal chains and cruel commands.

Circe landed beside her with a heavy thud, folding midnight-blue wings against the rain. Water shimmered across the dragon’s silver scales like liquid moonlight.

“You’re thinking too loudly again,” Circe said through their bond.

Keila smirked faintly. “And you’re eavesdropping again.”

“I prefer protecting.”

The dragon’s glowing eyes narrowed toward the palace courtyard below. Soldiers carrying crimson banners marched through the gates while servants rushed to prepare for the Festival of Bonding.

Keila’s stomach twisted.

Today marked exactly one year since she had been banished from the kingdom. One year since her parents declared her unworthy of the throne. One year since she chose Circe instead of the massive golden war dragon selected for her by the royal council.

The memory still burned.

“You embarrassed our family,” her father had shouted before the entire court. “A future queen does not choose weakness.”

Weakness.

Keila glanced at Circe and almost laughed at the absurdity of the word. Circe was smaller than most battle dragons, yes—but her speed could outrun storms, and her intelligence rivaled ancient scholars. She could read forgotten languages carved into ruined temples. She understood battle strategy better than generals.

And unlike the royal dragons, Circe chose her rider freely.

That frightened the kingdom more than anything.

A bell rang through the palace grounds below. Once. Twice. Three times.

“The ceremony begins,” Circe murmured.

Keila reached into her pocket and felt the tiny scroll hidden inside her cloak. Even through the fabric, the parchment felt warm. Dangerous. Alive.

Inside the scroll was evidence capable of destroying the royal council forever. Names. Secret alliances. Records of forbidden magic.

Proof that Lady Morvane controlled the throne from the shadows.

Keila had stolen it three nights ago from the hidden archives beneath the palace library. Since then, assassins had hunted her across forests, mountains, and abandoned ruins. Two villages had burned because people helped her escape.

Thousands were already suffering because of the kingdom’s lies.

And now she had returned.

Lightning split the sky.

Far below, nobles in jeweled masks gathered in the courtyard while musicians played soft melodies. The entire kingdom believed tonight celebrated unity between dragon riders and their beasts.

But Keila knew the truth.

The ceremony was a trap.

Another bell echoed through the rain. Then screaming followed.

Keila rushed to the roof’s edge.

At first she thought lightning had struck the courtyard. Guards scattered in panic while guests fled across marble steps. Then she saw them.

Shadow creatures.

Tall, twisted beings crawled from pools of darkness beneath the palace walls. Their bodies flickered like smoke, with glowing white eyes and claws shaped from pure shadow magic.

“The Veilborn,” Circe hissed.

Keila’s blood ran cold.

The Veilborn belonged to Lady Morvane alone. She only unleashed them when she wanted fear.
